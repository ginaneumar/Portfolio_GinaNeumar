Edeka Sustainability Check

To make our food sustainable too!

Overview

Background

Our task in my project is to design our own product in the form of an app, test it and go through the human-centered design process. Our product should serve the purpose of promoting sustainable consumption through digital product information; as a project of the Federal Environment Agency. I then decided after research for the supermarket "Edeka".

Because when you turn over a product, you don't clearly know how sustainable it is!

Goal

I set myself the goal of designing a sustainable version of an Edeka, where food should be searched for or scanned and then all sustainable information can be read.

Role

UX and UI Designer as well as Prototyper

Target Group/Persona

1 / 3
Sabine, middle-aged, is passionate about sustainability. Her own experiences have shaped her deep connection to animal welfare. Sabine is extroverted and social, motivating others to drive sustainable change. She seeks reliable information without endless research.
2 / 3
He is a young man who is neutral on sustainability. As an apprentice with a limited budget, he finds it difficult to implement a more sustainable life and often chooses the cheaper option. He would like to see more education and affordable options for effectively sustainable living. He was chosen as a candidate because he stands between the two extremes and can easily understand both sides.
2 / 3
an older gentleman who is less interested in sustainability. His priorities center on issues such as better working hours and more time with his family. He values traditions and is skeptical about labels. Technological developments cause him difficulties. Georg would like a user-friendly and reliable source of information.

I decided on 3 personas because everyone in this age group goes grocery shopping. But also each of these age groups has different needs as well as problems that i want to consider in the design.

Ideation

Sketches

I want to create an intuitive design for the smartphone. The 5 buttons at the bottom should make it easy to use. I have also first concentrated on the essential things that should be present in the design in any case: A history on the home page, a scan button to get all the sustainable information of the product that was scanned, an information page where it is exclusively about sustainability, seals or the like.

UI - Design

A new challenge was to make a good UI design for a few pages of my prototype.

Prototype

High-fidelity Prototype

In the high fidelity prototype you can already see how the app can actually look like. I developed a points system from 0.0 - 10.0 that shows how sustainable a product really is, while you can also read the individual details again.

I have designed several more screens when you open the app for the first time. You can log in or register first, but of course you can also continue without any registration. Here you can also see the designed logo that brings sustainability and Edeka together.

On the home page, you have Card that shows you your history of what you've searched or scanned before, so you can access it again afterwards. The cards can also be placed one below the other.

Under Search you can now see the correct categories and the last searched examples.

Under Information you can find information about sustainability in general and about the seals. Or an exact explanation of the sustainability scale that I have created.

The scan button is meant for barcodes, when you are in the supermarket you open the app and click on the scan button to get all the information about the sustainability of the selected product. You get information about sustainability: means origin, CO2 emissions, environmental / social sustainability and a Good to Know section. And finally you have a profile page (if you have decided to register).

Reflection

I also enjoyed working freely, realizing my own design ideas and achieving a good result in the end.
